# 11. Conclusion

This paper presents an artifact-backed prototype of generation-compatible, operator-compatible masked execution for decoder-only LLM inference and LoRA personalization. The design combines four ingredients: right masking at Linear boundaries that commutes with token-axis KV cache append, RoPE blocks, and grouped-query attention; operator-compatible nonlinear islands (paired permutations for activations and SwiGLU; orthogonal masks for RMSNorm; mean-preserving orthogonal masks for LayerNorm); dense Linear sandwiches with a boundary pad that randomize the centered hidden state seen at island boundaries; and a private LoRA training path that masks the adapter factors with a paired inner mask, masks the per-step backward gradients in the same right-masked space, and pads the LoRA rank with stronger dummy distributions while keeping loss and optimizer trusted-side.

We evaluate the prototype against (i) plain-reference correctness on GPT-2 and a modern decoder-only wrapper (RMSNorm + SwiGLU + RoPE + GQA), including prefill, decode_step, and greedy generation; (ii) adaptive proxy attackers — ridge / MLP inverters, signature / Sinkhorn permutation recovery, linkability AUC, LoRA adapter and gradient extraction, spectral-cliff and ensemble rank inference, and a cost-model timing classifier; and (iii) a local-emulation wall-clock benchmark. Across the 22 paper-side artifacts and the 19 correctness rows, the masked wrapper reproduces the plain reference token-for-token in the tested configurations; under the named proxy attackers, the full mitigation bundle keeps the worst-case classifier close to random chance for the items the artifact labels `low`; and the items the artifact labels `needs_more_evaluation`, `medium`, or `high` (notably spectral / gradient rank inference) are reported faithfully and not re-classified.

We are explicit about what this paper does **not** claim: no formal / cryptographic / semantic security; no real TEE wall-time; no full Qwen / TinyLlama / LLaMA LoRA fine-tuning; no PEFT / DeepSpeed / vLLM / FlashAttention integration; the padded LoRA rank itself is not hidden; loss and optimizer stay on the trusted side; hardware side-channels and a compromised TEE are out of scope. The `claims_mapping` document and the Limitations section pin every such boundary.

The natural next steps are: (a) integrate the trusted controller into a real confidential-computing TEE deployment and replace local-emulation wall-time with real measurements; (b) extend the LoRA training path to a full production fine-tune of Qwen / TinyLlama / LLaMA, integrated with a real framework; (c) introduce heterogeneous per-module padded rank so that the published `r_pad` is no longer the same across modules; (d) extend the proxy-attacker suite with stronger learned inverters and stronger rank-inference detectors so that the residual `needs_more_evaluation` / `high` rows of the rank inference matrix are either closed by construction or surfaced as open problems with a known bound.
