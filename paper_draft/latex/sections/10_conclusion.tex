\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We present an artifact-backed prototype of generation-compatible, operator-compatible masked execution for decoder-only LLM inference and LoRA personalization. The design combines four ingredients: right masking at Linear boundaries that commutes with token-axis KV cache append, RoPE blocks, and grouped-query attention; operator-compatible nonlinear islands (paired permutations for activations and SwiGLU; orthogonal masks for RMSNorm; mean-preserving orthogonal masks for LayerNorm); dense Linear sandwiches with a boundary pad that randomize the centered hidden state seen at island boundaries; and a private LoRA training path that masks the adapter factors with a paired inner mask, masks per-step backward gradients in the same right-masked space, and pads the LoRA rank with stronger dummy distributions while keeping loss and optimizer trusted-side.

The wrapper reproduces the plain reference token-for-token in the tested configurations across GPT-2 and a modern decoder-only wrapper. Under the named proxy attackers, the full mitigation bundle keeps the worst-case classifier close to random chance for items the artifact labels \texttt{low}; items labeled \texttt{needs\_more\_evaluation}, \texttt{medium}, or \texttt{high} (notably spectral and gradient rank inference) are reported faithfully and not re-classified.

We are explicit about what this paper does \emph{not} claim: no formal, cryptographic, or semantic security; no real TEE wall-time; no full Qwen/TinyLlama/LLaMA LoRA fine-tuning; no PEFT/DeepSpeed/vLLM/FlashAttention integration; the padded LoRA rank itself is not hidden; loss and optimizer remain trusted-side; hardware side-channels and a compromised TEE are out of scope. The claims-mapping appendix (\Cref{app:claims}) and \Cref{sec:limitations} pin every such boundary.

Natural next steps are: (a)~integrating the trusted controller into a real confidential-computing TEE deployment and replacing local-emulation wall-time with real measurements; (b)~extending the LoRA training path to a full production fine-tune of Qwen, TinyLlama, or LLaMA, integrated with a real framework; (c)~introducing heterogeneous per-module padded rank so that the published $\rpad$ is no longer the same across modules; and (d)~strengthening the proxy-attacker suite with learned inverters and stronger rank-inference detectors so that the residual \texttt{needs\_more\_evaluation} and \texttt{high} rows of the rank-inference matrix are either closed by construction or surfaced as open problems with a known bound.
